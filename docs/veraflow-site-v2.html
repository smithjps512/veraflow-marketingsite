The Veraflow Data Model

Five types of data. Only one you can act on.

Every supply chain platform mixes verified events with supplier estimates and manual guesses into the same data layer. When a delivery slips, nobody knows which number caused it. Veraflow classifies every field so you always know what you're looking at.

The rule: Scheduling, shipping, and quality decisions are only made from DECISIVE data. INFORMED data is always labeled as an estimate. DERIVED data shows its sources. The difference is never hidden.
Decisive
Physically Verified Events
Timestamped, system-recorded. Dock scans, confirmed counts, signed completions. The only data that drives decisions.
Informed
Supplier Estimates
Supplier-provided dates and quantities. Useful for planning. Always labeled. Never treated as fact.
Derived
Calculated Values
Computed from decisive inputs. Yield percentages, kit completion, projected shortfalls. Always recalculated from source.
Obsol
Program Risk Flags
Obsolescence and change events. Contract-governed. Alerts when unresolved. Hard blocks when no approved path exists.
True Yield
Field Reality
Net quality after customer returns with attribution. The number that tells you what actually worked — not what QC approved.

True Yield

The number your QC report doesn't show you.

Manufacturing yield tells you what left the floor. Final inspection yield tells you what cleared QC. True yield tells you what the customer kept — and used.

🎯
Attribution, not just counts
Every return is attributed: supplier defect, manufacturing error, design nonconformance, or customer error. Customer errors are excluded from true yield. The rest are assigned to the right party.
📊
Per-customer, per-PO, per-part
True yield rolls up to every level — a single part number, a full PO, a customer relationship, or your entire operation over any time period.
🔗
Supplier contribution visible
Material defects trace back to the supplier who shipped them. Over time you see exactly which suppliers are contributing to field failures — before it damages a customer relationship.

Supplier Scorecard

You told us 30 days. It's been 47.

Every supplier estimate is compared to the actual receipt. Date accuracy, quantity accuracy, incoming yield, and true yield combine into a composite score — calibrated per customer, not one-size-fits-all.

◈
Per-customer score weights
Different customers have different priorities. A high-volume customer may weight date accuracy at 40%. A defense prime may weight true yield at 45%. Configure once, runs automatically.
◈
Rolling historical accuracy
Every estimate a supplier makes is scored against the decisive receipt event. Over time the score self-calibrates — so you know exactly how much to trust the next estimate before you act on it.
◈
Field returns feed back in
Material defects returned by customers are attributed to the supplier who shipped the material. True yield is the most consequential score component because it measures downstream impact, not just on-time delivery.

Risk level: AMBER. PO-4821 (C62840-004, Curtiss-Wright) has two converging issues:

① Station 5 of 7 is active. Current station yield: 94.1%. At this rate, projected shortfall of 3 units against PO qty of 50. Station end timestamp will miss buffer by ~1.8 days. ② Supplier estimate (S-00) for the next batch showed est_arrival Mar 22, but historical date accuracy for this supplier is 62% — treat this estimate with low confidence. Recommend escalating Station 5 to floor supervisor today. If shortfall confirms at Station 6, initiate priority re-order.
What's the true yield on this PO so far?
True yield to date: 89.6% (27 units shipped, 3 returns). Attribution: 2 returns MATERIAL_DEFECT → Acme Precision Parts. 1 return MANUFACTURING_ERROR → internal. Zero CUSTOMER_ERROR returns on this PO. Supplier composite score updated to 74.
